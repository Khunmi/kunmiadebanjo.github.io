Ford GoBike Data Exploration

python, Numpy, matplotlib, seaborne and Pandas

INTRODUCTION

This project explores the individual rides in a bike-sharing system spanning the larger San Francisco Bay area included in the 'fordgobike-tripdata' dataset of over 180,000 records.

Dataset Description

There are approximately 175,000 bike rides in this data set with 11 trip attributes (duration_sec, start_time, end_time, start_station_id, start_station_name, end_station_id, end_station_name, bike_id, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender ). The member_gender and user_type features are categorical variables. Numeric data types are more dominant in the dataset with 5 variables being integer values.

Main Features of Interest

  • I'm interested in the time it takes for an average trip to be completed.
  • If any particular gender spends more time than the other on trips.
  • Another area of interest is the relationship between user_type and member_gender.

The 'duration_sec' variable will be of great importance in understanding average duration for trips. Other variables such as "user_type", "member_gender" and "member_birth_year" will also aid exploring relationships.

UNIVARIATE EXPLORATION

  • Duration

The visual output of cell 10 in the image shows an initial plot for the 'duration' variable in my dataset. Distribution plot of the duration variable has a long tail and it is rightly-skewed. Finding the right bin size for this ditribution took a while. Converting the variable from seconds to minutes or hours will be a good idea for me to explore.

After conversion of the Duration variable to minutes(cell output 11), a log scale histogram plot(cell output 14) was used to visually drill down into the distribution of the time spent during bike rides. There seems to be a few data points greater than where majority of the distribution lies(1- 100 minutes). An observed peak is seen at 10 minutes.

UNIVARIATE EXPLORATION

  • User Type

Another feature of interest for my analysis is the "user_type" variable. Exploring the distribution of this variable across the dataset shows that a majority of trips(80%) were carried out by the Subscriber user category while the Customer user category accounted for 10% of the trips taken as plotted in cell output 15.

UNIVARIATE EXPLORATION

  • Busiest Start & End Bike Stations

A seaborne countplot visual was used to determine the top five start and end stations with regards to how many bike trips started and ended in these stations repectively. Based on cell output 22, The top two busiest end stations are 'San Francisco Caltrain Station 2' and 'Market St at 10th St' with both of them switching postions for the busiest start stations plot. Considering the whole dataset, San Francisco Caltrain Station 2 is busiest bike station.

BIVARIATE EXPLORATION

  • Duration VS User Type & Gender Type

My bivariate explorations starts with investigating the time spent on bike rides with respect to different gender catgeories as well as User Type categories. Using seaborne's Violin plot, this reveals that a majority of the subscriber category members travel duration fall within the range of 1 to 10 minutes with over 50% falling below 10 minutes. The customer category have slightly over 25% of them with trip durations lower than 10 minutes while the rest fall above the 10 minutes cap. A more spreadout trip duration distribution is obeserved in the customer category type. The 'male', 'female' and 'other' gender types show similiar relationships with the trip duration variable regardless of their respective sample size.

BIVARIATE EXPLORATION

  • Categorical Vs Numeric Variables

I decided to investigate for existing patterns between categorical and numerical data types present in my dataset. A matrix of box plots was used for this visualisation. I started with storing all catgeorical and numeric features into two major variables ("numeric_var" and "categoric_vars") and then parsing them into Seaborne's ".boxplot" method.

Visual ouput from cell 27 informs that the average age of all gender and customer category is less that 40 yrs albeit several outliers present in the dataset. From the gender point of view, average trip duration is less than 10 minutes albeit several outlier present in the distribution. From the user_type point of view, average trip duration for Customer category is over 10 minutes while that of the Subscriber category are less than 10 minutes.

MULTIVARIATE EXPLORATION

  • Gender Activity Across Top 5 Busiest Start Stations

For multivariate visualisation, I decided to work with three major variables in my dataset "member_gender","start_station_name" and "duration_mins". Using the "member_gender" as hue for my Seaborne barplot, notable insights show that more male gender type in the dataset doesn't directly interprets to a dominance in all variables, this came as a surprise. Here in the top 5 busiest start stations, it can be seen that the Other gender category keeps the 'San Francisco Caltrain Station 2' and 'Powell St BART Station (Market St at 4th St) stations the busiest in terms of trip durations at start stations.

CONCLUSIONS

  • Average trip duration for the entire dataset hovers around 10 minutes. Most people start their trips for the day at 8am while evening trips are mostly started at 5pm. A percentage of 75 male gender type dominate the data set and majority (95%)of the bike riders are Subscriber category user types. Over 50% of the Subscriber category users travel less than a total of 10 minutes while the Customer user types record more minutes during bike trips.
  • Users of age range 32 to 35 ride bikes the most and average age across all categories is below 40yrs.
  • A surprising outcome is that the Other gender category took longer trips at some of the busiest stations, despite it being of the lowest percentage of gender type present in the dataset.
