Movie Rental Database Query - SQL, PostgreSQL and Tableau

The dataset for this project came readily clean and is easily accessible here.

  • Query 1 - Using PostgreSQL, this Query generates the top 10 family-friendly movies titles based on their respective rental count. Observations from Tableau generated visuals informs that the top ten family-appropriate movies range between 30 t0 32 total rental counts. Movie titles and subcategories are also provided for the purpose of recommendation.
  • Query 2 - I decided to write a Query to investigate each rental store's performance for the months of February, March, April and May for year 2007. The clustered bar chart generated by Tableau helps to underatand each store's performance at a glance. As seen, Store ID 1 outperforms Store ID 2 except in the month of April 2007.

Movie Rental Database Query - SQL, PostgreSQL and Tableau

  • Query 3 - More insights was drawn from the data set provided by querying the top five paying customers in the database. Top five customer names, monthly expense on DVD rentals and the total count of movies rented in each months. Tableau helps to visualise all these insights at a glance. per customer. As seen, Eleanor Huntmade the highes cummulative payment($100.78) in the month of April 2007.
  • Query 4 - For the top 25 horror movies according To Rental Duration, how do they compare to the rental duration of the entire movie Inventory In terms of quartile distribution? From the Tableau visualisation, a quartile distribution grouping shows the relationship between their rental distribution categories and that of the entire movie inventory. Majority(14) of the top 25 Horror movies fall in the fourth quartile of the entire rental duration in the ‘dvdrental’ Database.

Excel Dashboard Project-

The data source for this project was Kaggle. A ficticious sales data for a clothing store. I engineered an extra feature named "age category". Some of the insights generated from this dashboard include:

  • Based on the product purchase chart, females dominate all categories of product purchase from this store.
  • Revenue generated in year 2021 is more than that of 2020. The Covid 19 pandemic definitely affected sales.
  • The middle aged customers (40-49 yrs) spent the most amount of money in year 2020 while younger people aged 20 to 39 spent more in 2021 as observed via the dashboard.
